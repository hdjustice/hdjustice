# Marine MVP Initiative Brief — Comparative Analysis
*Claude vs. Copilot vs. Gemini*

---

## Bottom Line Up Front

The **Claude brief** is the most suitable for sharing with stakeholders who are knowledgeable about the marine domain but new to the product owner role. All three have unique strengths worth borrowing, and all three have meaningful gaps.

## Overall Verdict

### Claude — Best for Stakeholder Sharing
Well-structured, accessible-first, appropriate depth throughout. Missing real resource links and a "before" screenshot.

### Copilot — Best for Internal PM Use
Most thorough on risks and research planning. The strategic questions section is genuinely valuable. Too long and internally-oriented for new product owners.

### Gemini — Incomplete
Closest to the real project (has actual links, screenshot, GitLab context). Too thin for stakeholder alignment without major additions.

---

## Why the Claude Brief Wins for This Use Case

Your audience — stakeholders who know the marine domain but are new to the product owner role — needs a document that teaches the *shape* of a product brief while also being self-contained enough to orient them without a handholding session.

The Claude brief does this best because it:

- Uses consistent structure from the template
- Integrates accessibility considerations throughout, not as an afterthought
- Explains the "Won't have" rationale clearly (critical for new POs who often struggle to hold scope)
- Ties everything back to the NWS mission

For someone learning what a product brief is for, the Claude brief models the reasoning, not just the outputs.

---

## What the Claude Brief Is Missing

### From Gemini: Real project artifacts
The Gemini brief was written by someone with actual access to the project's existing documents. It includes a real screenshot of the current marine page, live links to the GitLab epic, Figma designs, and the product outline. That "Before" screenshot alone is worth a thousand words — seeing the current state immediately grounds the problem statement. The Claude brief only has a URL. **Pull that screenshot and those resource links in before sharing.**

### From Copilot: Strategic questions
The Copilot brief includes six strategic questions at the end (e.g., "Should the primary UX be search-first or map-first?"). For new product owners, framing open questions is a genuinely useful tool for getting them into a discovery mindset rather than just reading and nodding. This section is worth migrating into the Claude brief as an "open questions" or "decisions needed" block in the Discovery section.

### From Copilot: Milestone detail
Copilot has the most detailed milestone table with named phases, which gives new POs a mental model for how the work will progress. The Claude brief's phase list is correct but sparse.

---

## Where Gemini Falls Short

Gemini reads more like a working notes document than a stakeholder-ready brief. Specifically:

- **No undesired outcomes** — a critical thinking exercise for new POs
- **No accessibility mention** — a significant gap given the team's Section 508 mission
- **Non-standard KPI framing** — "AIM score of 9.5/10" would confuse new stakeholders rather than orient them
- **No go-to-market section** and no stakeholder list beyond the immediate team

Its brevity is a liability here, not an asset.

---

## One Gap All Three Share

None of the briefs establish a baseline for the KPIs — they all say "TBD." For a stakeholder document, even rough placeholder baselines (e.g., *"current marine page bounce rate estimated at ~60% based on comparable legacy gov pages"*) would be more useful than blank cells.

New POs won't know to ask for baselines if the brief doesn't model why they matter. This is worth flagging when you share the brief: **we need to pull current analytics before these KPIs are meaningful.**

---

## Recommendation

Start with the **Claude brief** as your base. Then:

1. Add the "Before" screenshot and GitLab/Figma resource links from the Gemini brief
2. Fold Copilot's strategic questions in as an "open decisions" section within Discovery
3. Flesh out the milestone table with named phases

That composite gives you a brief that's structurally sound, accessibility-first, and genuinely useful for onboarding new product owners.
