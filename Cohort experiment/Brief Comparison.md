## **RECOMMENDATION**

**Use your GitHub version as the PRIMARY brief** because:

1. **Government context requires depth** — detailed accessibility and risk assessment protects you if decisions go wrong
2. **Stakeholder alignment** — comprehensive version prevents "I didn't realize..." surprises later
3. **Your PM philosophy** — accessibility-first and inclusive design shine through; the test brief glosses over it
4. **Quality over speed** — as a PM, you need documentation that reflects strategic thinking, not just tactical checklists

**BUT** — consider creating a **1-page executive summary** (like the test brief) for:
- Quick verbal updates
- Elevator pitches to leadership
- Stakeholder discussions before deep dives

**Keep test brief for reference** of:
- Your concrete 2026 timeline
- Embedded resource links (add those to your version)
- "Key Decisions Made" section (your version doesn't have this — might want to add it)

**Bottom line:** Your GitHub version is the working brief for real design work. The test brief is useful for quick status updates, but it's incomplete for the level of rigor government products need.
