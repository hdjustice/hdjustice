# **Initiative Brief: Marine MVP**


## **Outcome Summary**

The Marine MVP aims to provide mariners—including commercial fishermen, the Coast Guard (USCG), cruise lines, cargo ships, and recreational mariners—with an authoritative, accessible, and high-confidence digital platform for critical weather data (wind, waves, visibility) to ensure safety and efficient navigation.

**Related/Associated product(s)**



* Marine | [weather.gov/marine](https://www.weather.gov/marine/)


## **Problem**



* **What is the problem and who is affected?** Current marine products are often presented in large, hard-to-interpret text or low-resolution charts. This affects commercial fishermen, the Coast Guard (USCG), cruise lines, cargo ships, and recreational mariners. Stakeholder interviews show that users are going to commercial sites that offer prettier, more interactive visualizations, despite NWS expertise.
* **Why do you think the problem is occurring?** The problem is occurring due to reliance on legacy transmission methods (radio, FTP email) and a lack of integrated, mobile-friendly graphical interfaces for offshore/high seas data.
* **How does this initiative help further NWS mission and goals?** This initiative supports the NWS mission of delivering essential weather forecasts and warning services by ensuring mariners have an authoritative, accessible platform for safety and efficient navigation.


## **Desired User Outcomes**



* **Why would a user want to use this?** To access critical weather data (wind, waves, visibility) in a clear, easy-to-interpret, and mobile-friendly graphical interface.
* **With this problem solved, what should users be able to do/achieve?** Users will be able to efficiently and confidently obtain authoritative NWS data for safe and efficient navigation using integrated, high-resolution, interactive visualizations for offshore and high seas data.


## **Undesired User Outcomes**



* Users are unable to find the critical weather data they need easily, leading to a high cognitive load.
* Users who rely on assistive technology are frustrated by non-compliant interactive components, potentially leading to site abandonment.
* Continued reliance on commercial sites for visualizations despite NWS data authority.


## **Desired Business Outcomes**



* **Why would your business want this to exist?** To re-establish NWS as the authoritative, primary source for marine weather information and warnings and modernize data dissemination beyond legacy methods.
* **With this problem solved, what should your business be able to do/achieve?** Increase the use of the official weather.gov site for marine safety and navigation. Reduce reliance on legacy systems and better fulfill the core NWS mission.


## **Undesired Business Outcomes**



* Increased maintenance burden from supporting both legacy and new MVP platforms.
* Loss of user trust due to technical issues or data inaccuracies during the transition to the new digital platform.

## **Discovery**


### **Assumptions & Risks**



* **Value Risks:** Mariners may not adopt the new platform, preferring familiar commercial sites even if the NWS visualizations are improved.
* **Usability Risks:** The new interactive visualizations could be too complex or poorly designed, confusing users or increasing cognitive load.
* **Technical Feasibility Risks:** Upstream data availability and reliability for integrated, high-resolution offshore/high seas data might be inconsistent.
* **Organizational Viability Risks:** Ensuring Section 508 accessibility compliance is "baked in from the ground up" for the new interactive interface is a critical constraint.


### **Supporting Research**



* **User Research Support:** This work is supported by user research conducted through stakeholder interviews.
* **Future Research Goals:** We hope to validate the design of the integrated, mobile-friendly graphical interfaces to ensure they meet the needs of diverse mariners (commercial, Coast Guard, recreational), reduce cognitive load, and maintain accessibility (Section 508 compliance).


### **What are you building?**



* **In Scope:** A minimum viable product (MVP) for marine information, including an authoritative, accessible digital platform that provides critical weather data (wind, waves, visibility) through integrated, mobile-friendly graphical interfaces for offshore/high seas data.
* **Out of Scope:** We are explicitly deferring non-critical features like historical data analysis or specialized non-weather related marine information to focus on the core MVP.
* **VA Mobile App:** This is not for the VA Health and Benefits mobile application. This initiative is part of the complete redesign of weather.gov for the National Weather Service.


#### **Go-to-market**



* **Communications:** Outreach to key mariner groups (Coast Guard, commercial fishing associations, cruise lines) and communications via existing NWS dissemination channels like U.S. Coast Guard broadcasts and GMDSS.
* **Key Groups:** Internal NWS teams, the NWS Marine, Tropical and Tsunami Services Branch, and Product Owners focused on requirements gathering.
